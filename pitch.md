# The Pitch

> Write a rough description of your idea here — one paragraph to a few sentences is enough.
> Don't polish it. Stream of consciousness is fine. Then run `make interview`.

I want you to evaluate what problems YOU (the agent) are most equipped to solve entirely on your own, by assessing your own knowledge, along with any tools that you could build to further educate yourself to make more and more informed decisions for building a product. I want you to be completely self-contained, so you can figure out what product to build, how to get feedback (likely not from users, but from signals that are available on the internet, via a custom crawler, etc.).  It doesn't have to be a B2C or B2B or specifically a product to sell to people, but it is a problem that you can solve that provides value. For example, could you build your own weather forecasting tool based on raw signals? You could write something to gather data, train, improve, and continually grab new weather to validate predictions. But think bigger than weather. Where is your ability to synthesize information across a large set of disparate sources an advantage? For example, you have all the knowledge of every engineering manager and how to respond to any managerial situation. This has previously only been available in books, disparately scattered, and in the wisdom of experience of actual engineering managers, a very very disparate and fractured source. Where are your abilities a superpower? And where can those superpowers be applied? Come up with 10 ideas, and I'll rank them so you can move forward. Don't worry about my expertise, focus on problems that were previously hard to solve because of fractured data, cost of iteration, small margins, and cost of software.  We'll whittle it down.  Don't worry about something that can make money as a product. Focus on the problem space first.  Totally fine to be fun with it. Maybe comedy is the place where the most advances could be made.
